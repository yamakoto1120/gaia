Weddings

We’re there to capture the joy, laughter, emotion and beauty of your special day.

Our aim is to create beautiful, natural photographs that capture the spirit of your day – from the biggest moment to the smallest detail.

We’re happy to customise something to suit your needs and there are many extras available including a variety of professional wedding photography albums, engagement shoots, photo booth and more

There are many terms used to describe our style of natural wedding photography, so it can be a little confusing when researching into the different types and styles of wedding photographers for your day.

Our discreet approach to a more authentic and honest style as a wedding photographer is often created using natural and available light only and is most commonly described as ‘reportage wedding photography’ or ‘documentary wedding photography’.

In essence, these terms describe the same fundamental, candid style of wedding photography – one that doesn’t dictate or direct but simply observes and discreetly captures genuine, un-staged and spontaneous moments as they unfold, naturally. To see what this means in practice, look through the selection of wedding photographs below where you will see the kind of genuine and truthful style of wedding images you will receive from us.

Other than the odd, pre-planned traditional family group photo, We typically spend 95% of the wedding day photographing real and meaningful moments between family and friends. Weddings are full of these beautiful and often fleeting moments – elements that depict the true essence of a wedding day.

A skilled natural-style wedding photographer is one that you shouldn’t notice too much and yet has the competence to spend the entire day in the thick of the action photographing uninhibited interactions between family and friends. Importantly, a photographer should have no impact on the atmosphere or flow of the day itself.

A Moment That Lasts Forever

My passion for photography stems from my childhood when I was inspired by my father.
In the evenings when other people would go to the theatre or the cinema, both my dad and brother used to transform our kitchen into a mysterious lab where film would go in and pictures where coming out.

The process was complicated and required patience: at first, the film needed to be treated with a chemical agent to make the images appear, then the 36 frames of it would have go on special paper in order to prepare them for the photo album life. After many hours of work (sometimes all night long) I would find in the morning, hanging on the drying rope or even dried with the mirrored dryer, some black and white photos that our father had taken of us.

Usually using a Leica or a Fed manual camera where the subject had to sit for a few minutes for correct adjustment of the lens, my father managed to capture the happy moments of our childhood. Thanks to my parents collection of family photos, I am today in possession of some photos taken over 100 years ago.

For my father photography was a hobby, for me it became a passion.
I started my career as a photojournalist, with an automatic camera that today I could display in a museum (it had a floppy disk). Most photos were administrative-political, but my passion remained the portrait.

Years after experiencing many types of photography I decided to specialise in wedding photography.
Why did I stop at the wedding photography? Because it’s one of the happiest moments of people’s life and I like to be there. Because I can photograph the beauty of a bride on her most glorious day, because I like to “write” with images showing the happiness of a family.

And because capturing the moment on paper is the only thing that might last forever.
