Weddings

There is a great variety of photography styles, that includes shooting in a relaxed, documentary, fashion or photojournalistic manner, and it can be potentially confusing when searching for the right one. We all want amazing pictures, but often when we are the subject, we find posing a little awkward, with the minimum amount of involvement required - the better.

That’s absolutely fine and you know why? It is because the pictures we appreciate the most, are actually the ones that have be taken with the heart. These pictures capture the fun, love, emotion and humour of the day and as result the photo will give you the same amazing feeling even if you see it hundreds of times again.

Our aim is to create beautiful photographs that capture the uniqueness of your day – from the biggest moment to the smallest detail. We are happy to customise something to suit your needs and there are many extra’s available including a variety of professional wedding photography albums, engagement shoots, a photo booth and more.

Our approach, like the weddings we shoot is unique; it can be a discreet, documenting the event with minimal intervention from the photographer or more traditional with perfectly organised group shots. Maybe something more artistic, modern, even vintage, each one can be brilliant as long as it captures the joy or your day.

For example, with natural-style wedding photography you shouldn’t really notice the photographers and yet they have the competence to spend the entire day in the thick of the action, photographing uninhibited interactions between family and friends.

For those feeling adventurous and comfortable in front of the camera, fashion is, perhaps, the style that highlights the glamour of the couple. Here, the photographer gets the chance to spotlight the beauty of the bride on her most glorious day or the last stag moments of the groom with his best friends; the hair, the makeup, the cufflinks, the Oxfords (not brogues) :) or a totally stunning dress’s features can be enhanced into a photograph that talks over time. Posing here is crucial, therefore the schedule should allow a bit more time for this type of photo shooting, which can be mixed with a different type (such as contemporary + fashion photography).

Understanding the basic principles of photography styles is important and the best thing when booking a professional photographer is to already know which style you love.

It’s a sort of a teamwork, where communication is the key of success. For instance, when you have seen a picture that you absolutely love, don’t ask the photographer to copy it but instead, try to explain what made you like it. For sure he/she will try and adapt it to the venue’s settings. Or if someone at your wedding celebrates their birthday and you have a surprise ready, make sure you let the photographer know so they can capture the moment.

As professional photographers, we can be that creative, innovative and up to date individuals that care and want to make your day special. We can suggest ideas you haven’t thought of and you might even like!

We have experienced almost everything, from the bride’s nervousness in the dressing room to the groom preparing for the first dance, which is why we encourage pre-event meetings. These allow us to visit the venue and to get to know each other better, giving all of us confidence and a chance to plan the day ahead.

Mini Photo Studio

We arrive before your guests and set-up a mini size professional studio. We’ll provide studio style lights and use either:

• A sparkly backdrop
• A white contemporary background

• A bespoke linen backdrop at your request
• An attractive architectural feature at your venue.

If your celebration is formal, we will take wonderful portraits. If your event is fun, we can bring a range of props and create an open photobooth. Or we can combine both elements – the choice is yours. Using a temporary photo studio we take glamorous, fun photos of your guests dressed in their formal attire. Prints can either be:

- Produced instantly for guests to take as a memento of the event

When we are asked to provide on-site printing for events, we produce deluxe quality prints that are instantly dry to the touch. The printed pictures are presented in an attractive photo mount which we then place in a protective bag.

- Sent by post to your office’s address

Guests can either purchase their prints on the night for an agreed price per item or the event organiser can pre-purchase a certain number of prints at an affordable cost paid in advance of the event. Following the event, we set-up a secure on-line gallery where guests can purchase re-prints or additional copies of their pictures.


Instant on-site printing at Charity Events

Using an Event Photographer that provides on-site printing is a proven way for charity events and charity balls to raise additional donations. We will happily donate up-to 10% of the print sales achieved on the night to the charity via the organisers.

Depending on the layout of your event it may be possible for the event photographer to also take pictures of your room setting and some roaming pictures during the entertainment. Just let us know if you would like this included when we discuss your event.

So if you are looking for professional and friendly event photography that provides guests with high quality photos and on-site printing contact us now to discuss your forthcoming event.

A Moment That Lasts Forever

My passion for photography stems from my childhood when I was inspired by my father.
In the evenings when other people would go to the theatre or the cinema, both my dad and brother used to transform our kitchen into a mysterious lab where film would go in and pictures where coming out.

The process was complicated and required patience: at first, the film needed to be treated with a chemical agent to make the images appear, then the 36 frames of it would have go on special paper in order to prepare them for the photo album life. After many hours of work (sometimes all night long) I would find in the morning, hanging on the drying rope or even dried with the mirrored dryer, some black and white photos that our father had taken of us.

Usually using a Leica or a Fed manual camera where the subject had to sit for a few minutes for correct adjustment of the lens, my father managed to capture the happy moments of our childhood. Thanks to my parents collection of family photos, I am today in possession of some photos taken over 100 years ago.

For my father photography was a hobby, for me it became a passion.
I started my career as a photojournalist, with an automatic camera that today I could display in a museum (it had a floppy disk). Most photos were administrative-political, but my passion remained the portrait.

Years after experiencing many types of photography I decided to specialise in wedding photography.
Why did I stop at the wedding photography? Because it’s one of the happiest moments of people’s life and I like to be there. Because I can photograph the beauty of a bride on her most glorious day, because I like to “write” with images showing the happiness of a family.

And because capturing the moment on paper is the only thing that might last forever.
